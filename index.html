    About Us

    Telugu language is considered as one of the most ancient and a sophisticated language. It is often lauded as ‘The Italian of the East.’ Telugu language is cherished with millions of stories, poems and novels. The word “CHAITRA” symbolizes to a new beginning. CHAITRA means Vasantham. Our club is a fresh beginning to promote importance of Telugu Sanskriti on the campus and around the world.

    The goals of the CHAITRA Association are as below

    • To experience the rich culture and heritage of Telugu nativity.

    • To provide a platform for students to improve the traditional values and to strengthen the coordination among all in celebrating Telugu festivals.

    • Organizing National and International Telugu Association Days by inviting guest speakers who have splendid knowledge about our Indian culture and Telugu nativity.

    • Organize programs like Rangoli, Games and celebrate all Telugu festivals and Telugu Cultural events in the Campus.

    • To embrace ethnicity and uphold the culture and traditions to future generations.

    The CHAITRA association aims to be alive and active by organizing events to engage the students in a happy and glorious way with fun-filled moments.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • Why should you work in Chaitra Telugu Association?

      The knowledge of mother language is the doorway to wisdom. We conduct events to help students find Telugu more engrossing. We work with strong team spirit which boosts productivity within all the departments and also increase team morale. Students are exposed to a proper working environment which develops their social skills.

    • How many club meetings will be conducted?

      There would be a monthly meeting on every 4th Sunday of a month. It is suggested for all the members of the association to join the meeting on time and also ensure good internet connection and meeting ambiance.

    • How do we perform grievance redressals?

      The Vice President and Secretary of the association act as the grievance in charge. Any grievance redressal can be done on 2nd and 4th Saturdays between (7pm to 7:45pm) in a month. In case of any action in favor of any other club ,or against the association by the club’s executive or non-executive core member including a member from the admin team, shall be liable to a serious action and their certificates will be held by the association’s governing body under the guidance of the faculty coordinator.
